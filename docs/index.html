Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know about NotchPrompter.

Is NotchPrompter free?
Yes, NotchPrompter is 100% free and open-source under the MIT License. No trials, no subscriptions, no hidden fees. You can optionally sponsor the project if you find it useful.
Does it show up in screen recordings or screen sharing?
No. NotchPrompter is completely invisible to screen recordings, Zoom, Google Meet, and all other screen sharing tools. Your audience will never know you're using a prompter.
What macOS version do I need?
NotchPrompter requires macOS Sonoma (14) or newer and works on both Apple Silicon and Intel Macs.
Does it work with external monitors?
Yes! NotchPrompter supports multi-monitor setups. You can place the prompter on any display; it doesn't have to be the one with the notch.
Do I need a MacBook with a notch?
No. While it's designed to sit beautifully in the notch area, NotchPrompter works on any Mac, including iMacs, Mac Minis with external displays, and older MacBooks without a notch. The prompter simply appears at the top of your screen.
How does voice-activated scrolling work?
NotchPrompter uses your microphone to detect when you're speaking. The text scrolls while you talk and pauses when you stop; so it naturally matches your pace without any manual controls.
